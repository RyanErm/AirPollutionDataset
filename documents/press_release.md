# How bad is the air you're breathing?


## What pollutants are in your air, and how does that affect the quality of the air that you are breathing?

## Since the onset of the industrial revolution, humans have been creating more pollution than ever before. This has been affecting many parts of the world, including the air that we breathe. Polluted air can cause a myriad of health problems, including cancer. Currently, the issue lies in determining which locations are at a high risk. This is important so that citizens can be warned about health hazards and so that further prevention work can be done there. To do so most effectively, we must have a objective, reasoned method to determine such locations. 

## Our solution! We have created a database of past air pollutant data that we have used to also devise a air quality prediction algorithm. By using numerical data on the environment (such as how much pollutants are in the air), we are able to predict the air quality for a specific location. This will help immensely in ensuring that residents or visitors are prepared for bad conditions by wearing face protection to avoid health hazards. This will also be useful in determining what cities/locations to focus further sustainability efforts on.

## Please look at the following visual to see current air quality rankings. Each dot represents a different city/observation. The dots are placed in order of their population density (people/km squared). The dots are also color coded by the air quality ranking for that one observation. This graph shows a possible correlation between the two features. The  main takeaway from this image is that Air Pollution is a problem for many. Analyzing the graph, it can be seen that many hazardous air quality rankings are for densely populated locations, meaning the health effects are felt by many there. This is a sign that we must take action on warning people to prevent health hazards and diverting more attention towards locations that we predict (with our model) have a hazardous air quality ranking.
